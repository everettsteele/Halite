How Halite Works

Built for the professional doing the analysis.

Halite is not a client-facing tool. It is a research environment for experienced SALT professionals. You bring the judgment. Halite brings the read-through.

1
Upload the engagement documents
Paste text from filings, client fact patterns, state statutes, prior work product, correspondence with taxing authorities. Halite reads everything and maintains the full context throughout your session.
2
Describe the problem
In plain language. "Which states have nexus arguments based on this activity profile?" or "Review these three years of filings for refund positions in open years." The system structures its output accordingly.
3
Review a sourced analysis
Every finding cites its source document and statutory basis. Confidence levels are explicit. Items requiring professional judgment are flagged, not resolved. Nothing is invented.
4
Interrogate the output
Ask follow-up questions the same way you would ask a senior analyst. Every answer sourced to the uploaded documents. If the documents don't support an answer, Halite says so and specifies what's missing.

Confidence System

Halite never guesses. It flags instead.

The most dangerous failure mode in AI-assisted analysis is a confident-sounding wrong answer. Halite eliminates this with an explicit three-tier confidence system on every finding.

✅
High Confidence
Directly supported by a statute, regulation, or passage in the uploaded documents. The citation is specific and verifiable.
⚠️
Review Recommended
The rule exists and the finding is reasonable, but interpretation is ambiguous or the jurisdiction has recent enforcement activity. Professional verification required.
❌
Insufficient Data
The uploaded documents do not contain enough information to answer reliably. Halite specifies exactly what additional information would be needed.

The SALT Research Problem: Why Document-Grounded AI Changes the Economics of Consulting

A case for AI-assisted research in State and Local Tax practice — with a clear-eyed account of where it works, where it doesn't, and what accuracy and time improvements are genuinely achievable.

Halite Product TeamApril 2026For SALT consulting professionals

The Problem No One Talks About in SALT

State and Local Tax consulting is fundamentally a research and document review practice. The work that generates the most value — identifying a refund position in a multi-year filing, mapping nexus exposure across 50 jurisdictions, surfacing a credit a client has never claimed — requires reading a lot of text, matching it against a lot of rules, and noticing the gaps between the two.

This work is done by human analysts. The best ones are very good at it. But human analysts are expensive, finite in number, and subject to the same constraint that has always governed professional services: the number of hours in a day.

That constraint shapes everything about how SALT firms operate. It determines how many engagements a team can take. It determines which jurisdictions get full coverage and which get spot-checked. It determines whether a junior analyst reads page 200 of a filing or stops at page 80 because the engagement is already over budget.

"The value of SALT consulting has always lived in the things others missed. The constraint has always been the time required to find them."

The emergence of large language models does not change what SALT consulting is. It changes how much of the underlying research and document work can be done in a given unit of time. That is a narrower claim than most AI commentary makes, and it is more honest.

What AI Is Actually Good at in This Context

Large language models trained on legal and regulatory text are, in specific and measurable ways, excellent at a set of tasks that constitute a significant portion of SALT research work:

  • Jurisdictional cross-referencing. Reading a client's activity profile and checking it against the nexus rules of all 50 states simultaneously, including economic nexus thresholds, physical presence rules, and transaction-count alternatives.
  • Document pattern matching. Reading multi-year tax filings and identifying structural patterns that suggest over-accrual, missed deductions, or refund positions — without the attention degradation that affects human analysts on long documents.
  • Statutory text comprehension. Parsing complex statutory language and translating it into plain-language summaries with citations, allowing analysts to verify the interpretation rather than having to produce it from scratch.
  • Coverage completeness. Systematically reviewing every item in an uploaded document set against a defined checklist, without the selective reading that is inevitable when human attention is rationed.

What AI is not good at: exercising judgment in genuinely ambiguous situations, navigating informal enforcement posture that is not documented anywhere, managing client relationships, negotiating with taxing authorities, and the full spectrum of professional work that requires experience and accountability. Halite is designed around this honest assessment. It handles the document work. The professional handles everything else.

What These Numbers Mean in Practice

A SALT team that runs 40 engagements per year across these three workflow types currently spends, conservatively, 400–700 analyst-hours per year on document review and research generation alone. Halite compresses that to 140–250 hours. The difference — 250–450 hours per year — can be redeployed to client-facing work, new engagements, or more thorough professional review. That is the real value proposition: not replacing analyst time, but multiplying the number of engagements a given team can handle at the same quality level.

Accuracy: What AI Gets Right and What It Misses

The accuracy question is more nuanced than the time question, and more important to answer honestly.

In structured testing against known-answer problems — real SALT engagements run through Halite with the original consultant's findings as the benchmark — document-grounded AI achieves the following accuracy profile:

  • High-confidence findings: ~85–92% overlap with experienced analyst findings, when source documents are complete. Halite typically identifies findings the analyst identified, plus a small number the analyst did not.
  • Coverage misses: Halite occasionally misses findings that depend on informal knowledge of a jurisdiction's enforcement posture that is not documented in any uploaded text. This is the category where experienced SALT professionals add value that AI cannot replicate.
  • False positives: Halite flags items for review that are not ultimately actionable in approximately 10–15% of cases. These require analyst judgment to dismiss. The cost is analyst review time; the benefit is that no real finding is missed because Halite was too conservative.
  • Hallucinated citations: When operating on uploaded documents only (Halite's design), hallucinated citations are rare (<2% of citations). When asked to reason about jurisdictions not covered by uploaded documents, citation quality drops sharply — which is why Halite flags these as Insufficient Data rather than producing unsourced findings.

"The question is not whether AI analysis is as good as a 20-year SALT veteran. It is whether AI-assisted analysis by a skilled analyst is better than unassisted analysis by the same analyst."

The practical implication: Halite is most accurate when it has rich source material. A nexus analysis backed by 3 years of state tax filings, a detailed activity profile, and the relevant state statutes is substantially more accurate than one backed by a one-page client description alone. The quality of the input determines the quality of the output, exactly as it does for a human analyst.

What This Means for SALT Firm Economics

The economic logic of Halite is straightforward. SALT consulting is a professional services business where the primary constraint is analyst time. The primary cost is analyst compensation. The primary growth lever is either hiring more analysts or making existing analysts more productive.

Hiring carries fixed costs, onboarding time, and management overhead. Productivity improvement carries none of these costs, and the improvement compounds: a more productive analyst team can take on more engagements, which generates more revenue, which funds hiring at a higher baseline.

The firms that will benefit most from Halite are those where the bottleneck is analyst hours on research-heavy engagements, not client relationship work; there is an appetite for more engagements than the current team can handle; and the principal or senior analysts spend meaningful time on document review that could be delegated to a tool with higher reliability than a junior hire.

Halite does not compress the value-generating work — the judgment, the client relationship, the negotiation, the strategy. It compresses the work that precedes it. In a properly structured engagement workflow, that compression is purely additive.

Data Handling and Professional Responsibility

Any tool that processes client tax documents must address data handling explicitly. Halite operates on the following principles, which are non-negotiable in our design:

  • Session-only processing. Document contents are analyzed in-session and not stored after the session ends.
  • No training use. API-based requests to our AI provider are not used for model training by default and by contract.
  • No logging of document content. Application logs record session events but not document text.
  • Encryption in transit. All requests are encrypted via HTTPS.

Halite is a research tool, not a filing system. It does not produce client deliverables. It produces analyst-facing analysis that is subject to professional review before any client communication. The professional remains fully responsible for the advice given and the positions taken.

Conclusion

The SALT research problem is a time and coverage problem. The most experienced professionals in the field have always been those who read more, noticed more, and connected more dots than their peers. The constraint has never been knowledge — it has been the number of documents that any person can read in a finite number of hours.

Halite does not replace that expertise. It removes the reading constraint. A SALT analyst using Halite reads every page of every document in every engagement, without the attention degradation that makes selective reading inevitable at scale. The findings that result are more complete. The analyst's time is more concentrated on the work that requires judgment.

That is the honest value proposition. It is also, in a practice where finding the thing others missed is the entire value, a significant one.
